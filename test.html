Investigating a Novel Biomarker for Alzheimer's Disease

The Role of the TrkA:p75NTR Ratio in the TgF344-AD Rat Model

SCROLL DOWN

A Progressive Decline

“Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is the most common form of dementia, affecting millions worldwide.”
“Hallmarks include Amyloid-β plaques and neurofibrillary tangles (Tau protein).”
“A key feature is the early degeneration of cholinergic neurons, critical for memory.”

The 7 Stages of Decline

The NGF Receptor Balance

“The survival of these neurons depends on Nerve Growth Factor (NGF), which binds to two key receptors.”

TrkA Receptor: Promotes neuron survival and growth.
p75NTR Receptor: Can trigger apoptosis when acting alone.

“Our study investigates if a declining TrkA:p75NTR ratio is an early indicator of neurodegeneration in AD.”

How We Will Test Our Hypothesis

🐀

Animal Model

“We will use the TgF344-AD transgenic rat and compare to WT rats at 6 age points (3–19 months).”

🧭

Behavioral Testing

“Assess long-term spatial memory (Barnes Maze) and working memory (Y-Maze).”

🧠

Neuropathological Assessment

“Quantify TrkA:p75NTR ratio on cholinergic neurons and assess Aβ plaque deposition.”

Anticipated Outcomes

“We expect a significant decrease in the TrkA:p75NTR ratio in AD rats as they age.”
This molecular change is expected to correlate with cognitive decline measured in the behavioral tests.
“This would support the hypothesis that impaired neurotrophic support contributes to AD pathogenesis.”

Why This Matters

“This research could provide deeper insights into AD mechanisms and support targeted therapies modulating neurotrophin signaling in early stages.”

“The data could fuel computational models to simulate interventions (e.g., activating TrkA or inhibiting p75NTR) to predict impacts on neuronal survival.”
