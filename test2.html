Investigating a Novel Biomarker for Alzheimer's Disease

The Role of the TrkA:p75NTR Ratio in the TgF344-AD Rat Model

SCROLL DOWN

A Progressive Decline

“Alzheimer's Disease (AD) is the most common form of dementia, affecting millions worldwide.” [5, 6]
“Hallmarks include Amyloid-β plaques and neurofibrillary tangles (Tau protein).” [15]
“A key feature is the early degeneration of cholinergic neurons, critical for memory.” [16]

The NGF Receptor Balance

“The survival of these neurons depends on Nerve Growth Factor (NGF), which binds to two key receptors.” [17]

TrkA Receptor: Promotes neuron survival and growth. [18]
p75NTR Receptor: Can trigger apoptosis when acting alone. [18]

“Our study investigates if a declining TrkA:p75NTR ratio is an early indicator of neurodegeneration in AD.” [53, 73]

How We Will Test Our Hypothesis

🐀

Animal Model

“We will use the TgF344-AD transgenic rat and compare to WT rats at 6 age points (3–19 months).” [25, 28, 29]

🧭

Behavioral Testing

“Assess long-term spatial memory (Barnes Maze) and working memory (Y-Maze).” [38, 45]

🧠

Neuropathological Assessment

“Quantify TrkA:p75NTR ratio on cholinergic neurons and assess Aβ plaque deposition.” [53, 55, 61]

Anticipated Outcomes

“We expect a significant decrease in the TrkA:p75NTR ratio in AD rats as they age.” [73]
This molecular change is expected to correlate with cognitive decline measured in the behavioral tests.
“This would support the hypothesis that impaired neurotrophic support contributes to AD pathogenesis.” [75]

Why This Matters

“This research could provide deeper insights into AD mechanisms and support targeted therapies modulating neurotrophin signaling in early stages.” [78]

“The data could fuel computational models to simulate interventions (e.g., activating TrkA or inhibiting p75NTR) to predict impacts on neuronal survival.” [79, 80]
